MatchOps helps you organize your coaching activities across multiple teams, seasons, and tournaments. This guide covers how to set up and manage your organizational data.

## Club Season Period

The app automatically calculates a club season period (e.g., "24/25") that is displayed with competition names. This helps organize data by playing year.

<Callout type="warning">
Configure the club season period in **Settings** before creating competitions. This affects how seasons and tournaments are named and grouped.
</Callout>

- The period is calculated from competition start dates
- Default is October-May if not configured
- Example: A season starting in November 2024 gets "24/25" added to its display name

See [Recommended Workflow](/guide/full-setup-path) for the complete setup process.

## Competitions

Competitions are either **Seasons** (for league play) or **Tournaments** (for cups and events).

### Seasons

A season represents a competitive period (e.g., "Spring 2025" or "Fall League 2024"). Seasons help you:

- Group related games together
- Track standings and statistics over time
- Compare performance across seasons

#### Creating a Season

<Steps>
<Step title="Open Seasons">
Open the menu (‚ò∞) and select **Seasons**.
</Step>

<Step title="Create New Season">
Click **New Season** and enter the required information.
</Step>

<Step title="Configure">
Set game format defaults and other options.
</Step>
</Steps>

#### Season Details

When creating or editing a season:

- **Name** - Descriptive name (club season period is automatically added)
- **Location** - Where games are typically played
- **Age Group** - U7 through U21
- **Game Type** - Soccer or Futsal
- **Gender** - Boys or Girls
- **League** - Use the 3-step dropdown: level (level 1, level 2, etc.), area (east, west, etc.), and series name
- **Period Settings** - Number of periods (1 or 2) and duration
- **Start/End Dates** - Season timeframe
- **Notes** - Additional information

#### Linking Games to Seasons

When creating a new game, you can associate it with a season. This:

- Adds the game to the season's record
- Updates season statistics automatically
- Includes it in season reports

### Tournaments

Tournaments are for cups, festivals, or single-event competitions.

#### Creating a Tournament

<Steps>
<Step title="Open Tournaments">
Open the menu (‚ò∞) and select **Tournaments**.
</Step>

<Step title="Create New Tournament">
Click **New Tournament** and enter the details.
</Step>

<Step title="Add Series (Optional)">
Add competition levels if the tournament has multiple tiers.
</Step>
</Steps>

#### Tournament Details

- **Name** - Tournament name (club season period is automatically added)
- **Location** - Venue
- **Dates** - Start and end dates
- **Age Group** - U7 through U21
- **Game Type** - Soccer or Futsal
- **Gender** - Boys or Girls
- **Period Settings** - Default game format
- **Notes** - Additional information

#### Tournament Series

Large tournaments often have multiple competition levels. Add series to organize them:

- **Elite** - Top competitive level
- **Kilpa** - Competitive level
- **Haaste** - Challenge level
- **Harraste** - Recreational level

Each series can have its own games and standings within the same tournament.

<Callout type="tip">
Use tournament series when your team plays in a multi-level cup where different teams compete at different levels.
</Callout>

## Teams

Teams represent your squads. Create a team for each group you coach.

### Creating Teams

1. Open the menu (‚ò∞) and select **My Teams**
2. Click **New Team**
3. Enter the team name
4. Link to a competition (optional but recommended)

### Linking Teams to Competitions

When you link a team to a season or tournament:

- Competition details auto-fill when creating games
- Statistics are tracked per competition
- Easy filtering in reports

### Multiple Teams

If you coach multiple teams (e.g., U12 and U14), each team can have:

- Its own roster
- Separate game history
- Individual statistics

<Callout type="tip">
When creating a game, select the appropriate team to keep your data organized.
</Callout>

### Team Placements

When editing a team, you can record final standings for competitions where the team has played:

1. Open **My Teams** and edit a team
2. Scroll to "Tournament & Season Placements"
3. Select placement (1st-10th) for each competition

Placements are displayed with badges:
- ü•á First place
- ü•à Second place
- ü•â Third place

These badges appear both in the team list and in competition views.

## Personnel

Track your coaching staff and team personnel.

### Adding Personnel

1. Open the menu (‚ò∞) and select **Personnel**
2. Click **Add Personnel**
3. Fill in the details

### Personnel Roles

Choose from 8 role types:

- **Head Coach** - Primary coach
- **Assistant Coach** - Supporting coach
- **Goalkeeper Coach** - Specialized GK training
- **Fitness Coach** - Physical conditioning
- **Physio** - Medical/injury support
- **Team Manager** - Administrative duties
- **Support Staff** - General assistance
- **Other** - Custom roles

### Personnel Details

For each person, you can record:

- **Name** - Full name
- **Contact** - Phone number and email
- **Certifications** - Qualifications (e.g., "UEFA A License", "First Aid")
- **Notes** - Additional information

<Callout type="info">
Personnel are assigned to individual games when creating or editing a game. They appear in game reports and Excel exports.
</Callout>

## Tips and Best Practices

### Recommended Setup Order

Follow this order for best results:

1. Configure club season period in Settings
2. Create seasons and tournaments
3. Create teams and link to competitions
4. Add personnel

### Naming Conventions

**Team names** should be simple - typically based on club colors or simple identifiers:

The app automatically shows context in parentheses when needed (e.g., "PePo Lila (Spring League 24/25)"), so you don't need to add age groups or years to team names.

**Competition names** should be descriptive but without the club season period - it's added automatically:

- ‚úÖ "Helsinki Cup" ‚Üí displays as "Helsinki Cup 24/25"

### Archiving

At the end of a season or tournament:

1. Review final statistics
2. Export any needed reports
3. Archive the competition
4. Historical data remains accessible
