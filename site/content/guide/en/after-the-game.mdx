After the final whistle, take a few moments to verify your data and capture any additional notes while the game is fresh in your mind.

## Post-Game Routine

<Steps>
<Step title="Verify Events">
Open the Stats modal and check that all goals are recorded correctly with the right scorers and assisters.
</Step>

<Step title="Add Notes">
Add any game notes - tactical observations, player highlights, or things to work on in training.
</Step>

<Step title="Quick Review">
Glance at the player stats table to see who contributed to scoring.
</Step>

<Step title="Export if Needed">
Save data externally for backup or sharing with other coaches.
</Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="tip">
Create a consistent post-game routine to ensure you capture all important information before forgetting details.
</Callout>

## Quick Stats Review

Click the **Stats** button to open the Statistics Modal. In the Current Game tab you'll see:

- **Score and game info** - Final result and match details
- **Goal events** - List of all goals with time, scorer, and assister
- **Player stats table** - Goals and assists per player
- **Game notes** - Your recorded observations

For detailed statistics analysis across seasons, tournaments, and player careers, see the [Statistics guide](/guide/statistics).

## Correcting Data

### Editing Goals

If a goal was recorded incorrectly:

1. Open the Stats modal (Current Game tab)
2. Click on the goal event you want to edit
3. Correct the scorer, assister, or time
4. Save changes - the score updates automatically

### Deleting Goals

To remove an incorrectly recorded goal:

1. Open the Stats modal (Current Game tab)
2. Click on the goal event
3. Click Delete and confirm
4. The score adjusts automatically

## Exporting Data

### Single Game Export

Export the current game's data:

1. Open the Stats modal
2. Click the Export button in the footer
3. Choose where to save the Excel file

The export includes:
- Game details and final score
- Complete goal event list
- Player statistics (goals, assists)
- Personnel assignments
- Game notes
